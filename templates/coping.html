Coping with COVID-19


Be kind to your mind


Mental health problems are common. Here are ways to cope with stress and promote wellbeing.


  • Take slow deep breaths, stretch, or meditate.
  • Observe how you are feeling and what you are thinking, without judgment.
  • Instead of responding or reacting to those thoughts or feelings, note them, and then let them go.
  • Meditation: In Depth
  • Make time to unwind.
  • Try relaxation techniques and listening to music.
  • Try to do some other activities you enjoy.
  • Exercise regularly. Even one session of moderate-to-vigorous physical activity reduces anxiety, and even short bouts of physical activity are beneficial..
    • Basics of Physical Activities
  • Get plenty of sleep. Be consistent. Go to bed at the same time each night and get up at the same time each morning, including on the weekends..
    • Tips for better sleep
  • Try to eat healthy, well-balanced meals. Add healthy fats, cut sodium, bump up your fiber, and aim for a variety of colors on your plate..
    • Healthy eating tips
  • Talk with people you trust about your concerns and how you are feeling..
  • Check in with your loved ones often.
  • It can help you and your loved ones feel less lonely and isolated.
  • If you, or someone you care about, are feeling overwhelmed with emotions like sadness, depression, or anxiety, or feel like you want to harm yourself or others, seek professional help.
  • If distress impacts activities of your daily life for several days or weeks, talk to a clergy member, counselor, or healthcare provider.
